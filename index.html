ABOUT

The Tour de Fraz started as a way for Jerry (aka Fraz) to blow off steam from his job at the hospital. He and his friends would go on hours long ride in the Chicagoland area. Fraz also organized longer bike-packing trips with a couple of friends through Kentucky and Wisconsin. The tours were typically 5 to 9 days long where the guys stayed at cheap hotels.

Fraz’s favorite bike trip was through Wisconsin along the Elroy-Sparta trail, part of the rails-to-trails network, featuring old railroad tunnels. He recalled one summer his new tires and tubes kept going flat. He called his wife, Vic, to come pick him up. On a Friday, Vic drove up to Wisconsin with her brother, Steve, to rescue Fraz. That night, they went out for dinner at a fish fry restaurant, however, the fryer was broken.

Once Fraz had kids and those kids were old enough to ride, the rides shifted from weeklong excursions to shorter day trips. Oftentimes, the rides were still along rails-to-trails in Illinois with easy turnaround points for tired legs. At the end of the ride, all the participants would join in a potluck lunch for sandwiches, sides and dessert. These yearly events became known as the Tour de Fraz.

UPCOMING RIDES

The next Tour de Fraz is tentatively scheduled for Saturday, September 14, 2025. We will try to keep to the second Saturday of September. We will ride along the same path as the previous year: starting in Frisco and turning around at Vail Pass. There is a good halfway point in Copper Mountain Village.

If you are interested in renting an e-bike, there are plenty of places in Frisco to rent for the day. All skill levels are welcomed to participate.

PAST RIDES

Inaugural - 2024

The inaugural Tour de Fraz - Colorado ride was on Saturday, September 14, 2024. We had 10 riders show up to honor Fraz’s name and legacy. The ride took place along Ten Mile Canyon Trail, starting in Frisco, CO, and ending at Vail Pass. It was a beautiful day with perfect weather.

Donations were collected and given to Parkinson’s Champions. Between funds collected the day of the ride and a company matching program, a total of $1,400 was raised for Parkinson’s research. This was an impressive giving from the group of riders.

CAUSE

In 2017, Fraz was diagnosed with Parkinson’s disease. His diagnosis hasn't stopped him from being an authentic, funny, phenomenal father and grandpa. In restarting the Tour de Fraz, the goal is to raise money for the Parkinson’s Foundation and celebrating and honoring Fraz’s name.

If you feel so inclined, please make donations directly to the Parkinson’s Foundation.
